Kudzu rewards the kind of shopping I trust: slow, opinionated, and a little suspicious of whatever is displayed too perfectly.

This is where a person can go in looking for nothing and leave with a jacket that improves every errand for the next ten years. The rooms hold furniture, lamps, art, clothes, dishes, and enough competing eras to make a clean aesthetic feel lazy. I like that. Taste gets stronger when it has to choose instead of simply match.

The trick is to let the first lap be reconnaissance. Notice color, weight, proportion. Touch fabric. Check pockets. Look under lampshades. Then circle back to whatever stayed in your mind after the louder objects stopped campaigning. A good vintage shop should make patience feel like a skill, and Kudzu does.
