Oakland is where I go when Atlanta has gotten too loud about becoming new.

It is not a spooky walk and it should not be treated like one. It is a place with names, fences, shade, brick, weather, and the kind of public memory a city cannot fully redevelop around. I like it on a Tuesday because Tuesday has less performance in it. The walkers are walking. The runners are cooling down. The stones are doing what stones do: outlasting everybody's theory of progress.

Give it time. Let the path bend. Read names without turning them into content. Then step back into the city feeling a little better proportioned.
