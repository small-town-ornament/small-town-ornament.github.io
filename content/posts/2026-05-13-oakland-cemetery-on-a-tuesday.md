There are places in Atlanta that ask you to slow down without making a production of it.

Oakland Cemetery does that better than most. The city keeps moving around it, impatient and half-dressed, but inside the gates the pace changes. Names, stone, shade, ironwork, the small humiliations of time. It is not spooky. It is specific.

I went on a Tuesday because Tuesday is when a city tells the truth about itself.
